Backtest System

It is a side project I wrote in my sophomore year in colloge using R and Shiny. Users can backtest their strategy on stocks online and get instant statistical results. There is alreay a built-in strategy so users can directly go to Backtest tab to try out. The system also offers advanced validation functionality called Walk Forward Analysis (WFA). After users setup the train and test dataset time interval, the strategy will be automatically applied to the time one day ahead of the interval iteratively. The results are collected and analysis report will be given. There is also chatroom that allows users to discuss with others. Other features are still in development but will be activated soon.

View Project

Recovery Rate Optimization

The project is from my half-year Data Analyst intern in PICK International Asset Management. The company’s main business was to bid on real estate property and randomly assigned it to a coordinator for resale. My task was to optimize the distributing process by matching certain types of cases to suitable coordinators to maximize profit. I used two-stage processing pipeline for this purpose. In the first stage, I used hierarchical clustering to retrieve features and classify the assets into five classes. Next, I implemented neural networks algorithm on each cluster in predicting the profits. However, compared to the high accuracy rate on train dataset, the accuracy rates on test sets were low. I did a quick research on the issue and found overfitting was the major cause for the problem. I used L2 regularization to impose a penalty on the size of coefficients. Hence, the potential of overfitting was reduced. As a result, the model achieved an 83% accuracy rate on the test sets in average.
